EDA Sprint 4-TECH-problem-solving

"blocked on a simple problem"

I would say in all honesty that I was blocked a lot on this set of challenges. However to name a particular problem, I would say that Fizz Buzz definitely presented a significant hurdle. I would also be the first to say that I understood what we were trying to tell the computer to do. Ergo, I understood the game. However the code required to achieve this, for me, was nowhere to be found. It was with much trepidation, extensive research and in no small part having a lot of help from our resident angel Johnny that I was able to blunder my way through Fizz Buzz. I don't believe elegant would ever be an adjective I would use to describe any of my solutions at this point. Ultimately, blissful would be the way I would describe my disposition upon completing this set of challenges, but in terms of reflecting on my technical prowess at this stage, I feel it is lacking…elegant problem solving seems a very distant achievement at this juncture. I learned fundamentally that I have a lot to learn...the journey continues.

reflect- problem solving techniques

-Pseudocode- I have begun to use this, it is helpful in that it keeps me on track and presents my desired outcome, I really want to get more confident with this technique.

-Trying something & reading error messages- this has for better or worse been one of my predominant forms of problem solving. The error messages if you take time with them can be very helpful in identifying holes and mistakes in your code.

-rubber ducky method- this has on occasion garnered me a solution to a problem. Just telling someone what is happening has surprising benefits when you are stuck. I would like to employ this technique more as I believe it helps to access latent knowledge I did not realise I have.

-console.loggin’- I find this to be super helpful. I'm getting more confident with using it as I move forward. Testing your code safely and regularly is invaluable

-googling- is without a doubt my most used problem solving technique. I get better daily at being able to articulate my problem and more and more I’m getting better results from my searches.

-Getting help from my peers- I have been so impressed and humbled by the helpful and exceedingly knowledgeable students I study alongside with. I have found them to be unendingly kind and helpful through this whole process thus far, it has been a joy and an honour to share it with them.

-getting help from facilitators-where to begin, they are saints. Truly helpful. The way they guide you to solutions is amazing, I often know more than I thought I did, and on so many occasions it has been a facilitator who has helped me to realise this.
